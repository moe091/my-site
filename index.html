I'm just some guy

I'm just some guy who spends a lot of time writing code. Like, A LOT of time, ever since I was 10 years old. Like many kids I got into gaming young, and like many gamers with a creative side I quickly built up a desire to make my own games. As an elementary school student I had no idea how, but I kept reading and researching and asking until I had it figured out, and that's how I got into programming. Before I even finished my first game though, I realized that I cared more about just writing code than making games. I explored other areas; web development, desktop software, mobile app development, and more recently AI/Machine learning and competitive programming. That's a pretty wide feild of interests, but as I said earlier I spent plenty of time writing code, practicing and learning new things. It's been over 15 years since I first started programming, and I've gained tons of valuable experience. Nowadays I mostly focus on web and mobile development for work, while playing with game development, AI, and competitive programming in my free time to keep things interesting while further improving my skills. Although my life has heavily revolved around software development for almost as long as I remember, you wouldn't know it unless I told you; As I said I'm just a guy who spends a lot of time writing code.

I'm someone you can trust to make your web or mobile app

As a freelancer, I take every project I work on very seriously. It's hard to show off how good your code is to the laymen, the only way to succeed in a business like this is to make your clients happy. Us software professionals love geeking out about the newest technologies and development tools, but it's important to understand that our clients have their own needs for hiring us and what they care about is results. A lot of freelancers and clients have issues communicating with eachother, staying on the same page, and issues often arrise from misunderstandings. As someone who used to run a brick-and-mortar business that had nothing to do with technology, I can understand the needs of my clients regardless of how technical they are. I can help them understand, in clear terms, the relevant technical details of their project, and I can listen carefully to understand exactly what they want and why they want it. I am very used to talking about technical things in non-technical terms, and this helps me create software that matches the vision of my clients and does as good a job as possible at fulfilling it's purpose.
