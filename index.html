What do I do?

In laymens terms, I make software. That includes websites, mobile apps, games, and desktop tools. I use code to build them, but that's only half the story. Any good software serves a purpose - whether it's a website to attract customers to your business, an app that solves a problem for users, or anything else. And creating anything good requires good design. I focus just as much on creating a good user experience and making my software attractive as I do on writing clean and robust code. I understand that my clients use software as a means for their own ends, and I want to create software that does the best job possible at serving their purpose.

As a freelancer, communication is also a big part of my job. I think the difference between an average freelancer and a great one is the ability to listen to and communicate with clients. It's important to understand your clients needs, because if you aren't on the same page you won't be able to create software that matches their vision and fulfills it's purpose to your clients. I am very good at bridging the communication gap with my clients regardless of their level of experience with technology, and as a result I tend to make software that they are very happy with!
